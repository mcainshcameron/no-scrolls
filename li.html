LLMsTransformer ArchitectureModel Training Stability
A new DeepSeek paper is highlighted for proposing an architectural refinement to Transformers called mHC (Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections). The post explains that while Hyper-Connections introduce multiple parallel residual “lanes” to improve information routing, unconstrained mixing across many layers can cause signal explosion or vanishing, destabilizing training at scale. mHC constrains these mixings to behave like a “safe” averaging operation, enabling stable training up to 27B parameters and improving performance versus both standard Transformers and unconstrained HC variants.
The Bottom Line
  • Hyper-Connections generalize residual connections by maintaining multiple parallel residual streams that are mixed via learned matrices each layer.
  • Unconstrained mixing can destabilize large models as depth increases due to signal amplification/attenuation.
  • mHC constrains the mixing to prevent runaway scaling of the residual signal, improving training stability.
  • Reportedly, unconstrained HC can amplify residuals ~3000×, while mHC keeps amplification to ~1.6×, with stable scaling to 27B parameters.
