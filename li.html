The post distinguishes “search” from “research,” arguing that deep research requires long-horizon planning, iterative tool use, cross-checking, reflection, and coherent synthesis. It summarizes Step-DeepResearch, an end-to-end single-agent approach trained on atomic capabilities (planning/decomposition, information seeking, verification/reflection, report writing) via a progressive pipeline (mid-training, SFT, and RL with rubric-based rewards). The claimed result is near state-of-the-art performance on research benchmarks with a 32B model at substantially lower inference cost, plus the introduction of ADR-Bench for more realistic evaluation.
The Bottom Line
  • Deep research is framed as a multi-skill process (planning, intent recognition, iterative tools, verification, reflection, synthesis), not mere retrieval.
  • Step-DeepResearch trains “capabilities atomiche” and shifts emphasis from next-token prediction toward action selection for agentic robustness.
  • A progressive training stack is described: mid-training for agent behavior, supervised fine-tuning, then RL with rubric-based rewards.
  • The work introduces ADR-Bench (Chinese, application-oriented, human-evaluated) and reports competitive results with a 32B model and lower inference costs.
