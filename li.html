BurnoutProductivityWork Culture
The post argues that modern burnout persists despite abundant automation and productivity tools because tools increase capacity rather than create true free time. That added capacity is quickly filled by higher expectations and faster cycles, turning efficiency into an end in itself. The proposed remedy is a mindset shift: simplify work instead of accelerating it, protect idle time, and measure productivity by intention rather than volume.
The Bottom Line
  • Automation often expands organizational throughput expectations, which can negate any personal time savings.
  • Over-optimization can drive exhaustion by compressing cycles (reply faster, ship earlier) rather than reducing workload.
  • Burnout mitigation requires explicit norms: protecting rest/idle time and aligning efficiency with purpose.
